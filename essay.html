Compare and Contrast Essay

In our world,it seems like there has been a boom of people coming out as a multitude of things. They are part of the LGBTQ+ community: lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer+. The debate is over the “Q+” in the LGBTQ+. Are there more than 2 genders, is gender a spectrum?
If gender is a spectrum, you could break it down into 5 different genders in its most basic form. At the ends of the spectrum, there are male and female, they are also known as 1st gender. The third gender can be described as a feminine male. The 4th gender can be described as a masculine female. 5th gender in the middle of male and female.
The main argument that has been made is that, if you are a male you have XY chromosomes and if you are a female you have XX chromosomes. With that ideology that excludes intersex people who have both male and female anatomy to varying degrees. There was a boom of intersex people in the 1880s and that was the introduction of the binary system. Doctors started pressuring people to have unnecessary surgeries to either be male or female.1 There have been examples of nonbinary people in history and religion.
Some Native American tribes had a multigender system, they have a ceremony that had children at the age of 8 to choose a basket or a bow. This would decide what they would for the rest of their life. If a child who was born a male chose the basket they were given a new name and female clothes and vise versa for a child who as born a girl and picked up the bow. In India, there is a term called Hijra. Which is the third gender, they believe a true hijra lacks a penis and scrotum and some have surgery to remove them. In Indonesia, they have a term called bissu, which is the 5th gender. This happens to someone when they learn a language through their dreams. Some believe that a true bissu don’t menstruate or get an erection. Because bissu have both masculine and feminine sides of the spectrum they are allowed to be possessed by dewata(gods).
On the other side of the spectrum, you could argue that gender isn’t a spectrum, that gender is binary. You could argue that just because 1% of the population is intersex doesn’t mean that gender is a spectrum. “There exist individuals who possess fewer or more than 10 digits on their hands, but this hasn’t called for a re-conceptualization of how many fingers a human being has.” Transgender people change their sex to the opposite gender still making gender binary and not a spectrum. Because of this rising culture of the gender spectrum, you have stories like David Reimer.
Reimer was a subject of an experiment in the 60s. He was raised as a girl and was put on female hormones. Doctors wanted to prove that feminine or masculine isn’t genetic, but that it is a social construct. But after learning that he was a male, he wanted to go back to his male identity. Sadly after all of his hardships, he committed suicide at the age of 38. 3 Saying that gender is a spectrum just causes confusion for the public and stories like David Reimer.

Commentary

The compare and contrast essay shows why gender is a spectrum and why it isn’t spectrum with valid points on both sides. The purpose was to debate the gender spectrum theory and show different perspectives. The audience is for people who debate if gender is a spectrum or not.
