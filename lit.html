Review: Nonbinary: Memiors of Gender and Identity

Scott Duane is a transgender man and he lives in Oakland California. Along with advocating for the LGBT community, he is a software engineer in San Fransisco. Micah Rajunov is a nonbinary person living in Boston with their spouse before working in San Fransisco. They have had other works published such as “Engaging Transgender Patients: Using Social Media to Inform Medical Practice and Research in Transgender Health.” and have been interviews for publications in the New York Times.
They are both strong advocates for the LGBT community and writing a first-person novel about the experiences of Nonbinary people to the general public as a way to educate people.
Nonbinary: Memoirs of Gender and Identity gives a first-person experience of exploring gender identity. Over the course of the book, you go through a journey of what one would experience when question their identity. You question yourself almost shy, then you evolve to be proud of yourself, then you have a sense of community, a safe space. Then it finally crescendos in the discussion of trans rights and the age-old question “Are there multiple genders?”
There are 5 different of the book respectfully named: What is gender?, Visibility: Standing Up and Standing Out, Community: Creating a place for the rest of us, Trans Enough: Representation and differentiation, and Redefining Dualities Paradoxes and possibilities of gender. I would recommend this book to someone who just wants more insight into the world of nonbinary people.
